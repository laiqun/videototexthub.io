Extracting text from images used to mean installing OCR software, tuning settings, and hoping the output was usable. Modern AI has changed that: upload a picture, and a multimodal model reads the text the way a person would — including layout, mixed languages, and imperfect photos.

This guide covers when text extraction helps, how to do it well, and how to handle many images at once.

## What Is OCR and Why Does It Matter?

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) converts text inside images into editable, searchable, copyable text. The text in a screenshot, a scanned contract, or a photo of a receipt looks readable to you — but to a computer it is just pixels until OCR turns it into characters.

Once text is extracted, you can search it, paste it into documents, translate it, feed it to other tools, or archive it properly. That is why OCR sits quietly behind a huge number of everyday workflows.

## Common Situations Where You Need It

- **Screenshots** — error messages, chat conversations, code snippets, or slides you want as real text instead of a picture
- **Scanned documents** — contracts, forms, and old paperwork that need to become editable files
- **Receipts and invoices** — pulling amounts, dates, and vendor names for expense tracking
- **Photos of physical text** — signs, menus, book pages, whiteboards, business cards
- **Social media images** — quotes and infographics you want to quote or repurpose

Traditional OCR tools handled clean scans well but struggled with photos: perspective, shadows, and decorative fonts broke them. AI-based extraction is far more forgiving because the model understands context, not just glyph shapes.

## Tips for Better Accuracy

- **Sharper is better.** High-resolution, high-contrast images give the cleanest results. If you can retake a photo straight-on instead of at an angle, do it.
- **Crop to the content.** Borders, UI chrome, and background clutter can confuse the transcription.
- **Mind the lighting.** Glare and deep shadows over text are the most common cause of missed characters.
- **Set the output language.** The tool can respond in your preferred language, which also helps it resolve ambiguous characters.
- **Review numbers carefully.** Dates, amounts, and IDs deserve a quick human check — they are where mistakes hurt most.

Clear handwriting is often recognized too, but printed text remains the most reliable. If a result looks off, try a tighter crop or a sharper image before giving up.

This is a meaningful upgrade over classic OCR, which typically required you to declare a single language upfront and still got confused by mixed content.
