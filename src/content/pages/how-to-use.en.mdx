AI Image Describer turns any image into useful text — descriptions, captions, alt text, AI prompts, or extracted text (OCR). This guide walks through everything you can do, step by step. No account is required to start.

## Step 1: Upload Your Images

Open the tool on the homepage and drag images into the upload area, or click to browse your files.

- **Formats:** JPG, PNG, and WebP
- **Batch:** up to 9 images at a time, each up to 20 MB
- **Sources:** photos, screenshots, scanned documents, artwork, and AI-generated images all work

Each uploaded image appears as a card with its own status, so you can track progress when processing a batch.

## Step 2: Choose What You Want

Below the upload area, pick a preset that matches your goal:

- **Describe in detail / briefly** — natural-language descriptions at your preferred length
- **Caption & title** — a short title plus caption, handy for social posts and metadata
- **Extract text** — OCR that pulls text out of the image while preserving layout
- **General / Midjourney / Stable Diffusion / Flux prompt** — reverse-engineer a prompt for AI image generators

You can also type your own instruction in the text box — for example, "describe the color palette" or "list every object on the table." Enable multi-select to combine several presets into one request.

## Step 3: Pick an Output Language

Descriptions can be generated in your preferred language — English, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, and more. Select it from the language dropdown before generating.

## Step 4: Generate and Review

Click the describe button (or press the keyboard shortcut) and watch the results stream in live for each image. When a result is ready you can:

- **Copy** a single result, or copy everything at once
- **Export** the whole batch as CSV or JSON — useful for alt-text and metadata workflows
- **Remove** individual results, or clear the list and start over

Results are also kept in your local browser history, so you can revisit recent generations without uploading again.

## Tips for Best Results

- Use sharp, well-lit images — the clearer the input, the better the output
- Be specific in custom instructions ("alt text for a product photo" beats "describe this")
- Match the preset to the task: OCR for text, prompt presets for AI art, detail presets for accessibility
- Review and edit generated text before publishing — treat it as a strong first draft
